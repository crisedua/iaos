## The Offer Architecture

### Layer 1: Free Challenge (Lead Magnet)

**Name:** "Challenge IA: Recupera 20+ Horas por Semana"
**Format:** 4 days, ~15 min/day
**Delivery:** WhatsApp group (primary) + daily Loom video or voice note
**Cap:** 50 participants per cohort (creates intimacy + scarcity)
**Dates:** March 10-13 (Mon-Thu)

**Why WhatsApp group (not email, not Telegram):**
- LatAm default communication channel — everyone checks WhatsApp hourly
- Group format creates community, social proof, peer pressure to complete
- User can see who's most engaged and DM them directly
- Voice notes feel personal and build trust faster than written content
- No tech friction — no app to download, no email to check

**Day-by-day content:**

| Day | Theme | Teach | Task (15 min) | Framework Source |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Tu Stack de IA | The 3 types of AI tools (chat, agents, automations). Why most people use AI wrong — they treat it like Google instead of like a team member. | Open Claude/ChatGPT. Give it one task you did manually this week. Compare the result. Share in the group what happened. | Martell: Buyback Rate — identify tasks below your rate |
| 2 | Tu Cerebro Digital | Why generic AI is useless but personalized AI is a superpower. Introduce the Context OS concept — your AI needs to know you. | Build a mini Context OS: write 1 paragraph each for (1) what you do, (2) how you work, (3) your #1 goal this quarter. Paste it into Claude and ask it to plan your next week. Share the result. | IA OS Layer 1: Context — the unique IP |
| 3 | Tu Primer Agente | How to identify the right task to automate (DRIP). Turn your Context OS into a working agent that handles a real recurring task. | Pick your most repeated weekly task (DRIP test). Write a prompt using your Context OS. Run it. Post in the group: "automated [X] — saved [Y] minutes." | Martell: DRIP + IA OS Layer 4: Automatización |
| 4 | Tu Hoja de Ruta + Oferta | The Director vs. Operator mindset. Why systems beat hustle. Your 90-day AI roadmap. Then: the Sprint offer. | Map your top 5 time-draining tasks. Score each by DRIP. You now have your automation roadmap. For those who want help implementing: Sprint Director IA. | Martell: Operator Trap + Hormozi: Grand Slam stack |

**Key design principles:**
- Each day builds on the previous day (Context OS from Day 2 feeds Day 3 and 4)
- Tasks produce shareable artifacts (social proof in the group)
- Day 4 is both the culmination AND the natural transition to the paid offer
- The challenge itself is the first step of the paid engagement (Hormozi lead magnet principle)

### Layer 2: High-Ticket Upsell

**Name:** "Sprint Director IA"
**Price:** $1,500 USD (one-time) — or 2 payments of $800
**Duration:** 2 weeks (compressed delivery)
**Format:** 3 live sessions (Zoom) + async build between sessions
**Cap:** 5 spots per cohort (scarcity + delivery capacity)

**Why $1,500 (not $900 Sistema, not $250 Starter):**
- $900 Sistema is 90 days — too slow for the urgency the challenge creates
- $250 Starter is too light — challenge participants want the full system
- $1,500 = compressed Sistema value in 2 weeks instead of 90 days
- Sprint framing = speed + done-for-you = higher perceived value
- 3-4 closes at $1,500 = $4,500-$6,000 (hits $5K target)
- Payment split option (2×$800) reduces friction for LatAm buyers

**What they get (Grand Slam stack):**

| Their Obstacle | What's Included |
|---|---|
| "Don't know where to start" | Session 1: Deep discovery + complete Context OS built for them |
| "I built something in the challenge but it's basic" | Full Context OS with all 9 sections (vs. the 3-paragraph mini from Day 2) |
| "I don't know what to automate" | DRIP audit of their entire operation — 3 highest-ROI automations identified |
| "I can't build automations myself" | We build all 3 automations for them — tested with real data |
| "Will I use this after the sprint?" | Weekly review template + Director Manual — their operating rhythm |
| "What if I get stuck?" | 30 days async WhatsApp support post-go-live |
| "I need to produce outputs, not just organize" | 3 output templates (proposals, emails, reports) customized to their voice |

**Delivery timeline:**
- Session 1 (Day 1, 90 min): Discovery + Context OS build
- Async build (Days 2-5): 3 automations + output templates
- Session 2 (Day 6, 60 min): Demo automations, train, adjust
- Async refinement (Days 7-10): Polish + edge cases
- Session 3 (Day 11, 60 min): Go-live — full solo run + Director Manual handoff
- Post-sprint: 30 days async support via WhatsApp

**Value anchor (use in Day 4 pitch):**
> "Si dedicas 10 horas por semana a tareas que podrían automatizarse, y tu hora vale $50, estás perdiendo $2,000/mes. El Sprint cuesta $1,500 una vez y te devuelve esas horas permanentemente. Se paga solo en 3 semanas."

**Upsell after Sprint:** Director retainer at $350/month (existing tier). Mention but don't push during the Sprint.

## Design Decisions

### Key Decisions Made

1. **WhatsApp group delivery (not email):** LatAm audience checks WhatsApp hourly. Group creates community and social proof. Voice notes build trust faster than written emails. No tech friction.

2. **$1,500 Sprint (not $900 Sistema):** The challenge creates urgency for speed. $900 over 90 days feels slow after 4 days of momentum. $1,500 for 2 weeks = compressed value = higher perceived worth. Only need 3-4 closes for $5K.

3. **50-person cap:** Creates real scarcity (fill it, don't fake it). Keeps the group intimate enough for meaningful engagement. Prevents the group from becoming noisy and impersonal.

4. **5 Sprint spots:** Realistic delivery capacity for one person. Creates urgency ("solo 5 cupos"). Enough for $7,500 if all close (buffer above $5K target).

5. **Loom videos OR voice notes (not polished video):** First cohort is about validation, not production quality. Voice notes feel more personal and are faster to produce. Loom screen shares can show real demos. Polish comes in cohort 2.

6. **Day 4 offer is in the group (not a separate webinar):** Keeps momentum. No extra signup step. Everyone who completed Day 4 sees the offer naturally. Lower friction than scheduling a separate event.

7. **Diagnostic call before close (not direct purchase):** LatAm buyers need personal connection. The call qualifies them and personalizes the proposal. Close rate from diagnostic is significantly higher than from a checkout page. Follows existing `ia-os-intake.md` flow — no new process needed.

8. **March 10-13 timing:** Gives 10 days to promote (Mar 1-9). Gives 18 days post-challenge to close and start delivery (Mar 14-31). Avoids starting too early (not enough promo time) or too late (not enough close time).

### Alternatives Considered

| Alternative | Why Rejected |
|---|---|
| Cold WhatsApp outreach to 968 leads | Ban risk. User explicitly flagged this. Also: cold outreach to businesses ≠ IA OS Director ICP (those leads are for SolIA). |
| Email-delivered challenge | LatAm open rates for email are low (15-20%). WhatsApp open rates are 90%+. No comparison. |
| Paid ads to fill challenge | User has no ad budget. Organic only. Can add paid in cohort 2 if cohort 1 validates. |
| Webinar instead of challenge | 4-day challenge builds more trust than a 60-min webinar. Completion = self-qualification. Challenge participants have already USED the framework before buying. |
| Lower price point ($500-$900) | Need 6-10 clients to hit $5K. Too many closes for first cohort with no case studies. $1,500 × 3-4 is more achievable. |
| US market (English) | No existing audience. Building from zero in 4 weeks = too risky. LatAm: has personal network, WhatsApp communities, LinkedIn connections. Can add English cohort in April. |

### Open Questions

1. **Video vs. voice notes:** Should the daily content be Loom screen-share videos (more polished, reusable) or WhatsApp voice notes (faster to produce, more intimate)? Recommendation: voice notes for cohort 1, upgrade to Loom for cohort 2.

2. **Payment method:** What payment methods to accept? Transferencia bancaria works for Chile. PayPal/Wise for other LatAm countries. Need to confirm what's set up.

3. **Challenge date flexibility:** March 10-13 is the plan. If promotion is slow by Mar 7 (<25 signups), consider pushing to March 17-20 and extending promotion by a week. Revenue still achievable by Mar 31 but tighter on close time.

## Notes

- **Cohort 1 is a test.** The goal is $5K AND validation. If it works, run monthly cohorts (cohort 2 in April with Loom videos, maybe paid ads, higher price).
- **The challenge IS the case study.** Participant results from the 4 days become testimonials and social proof for cohort 2.
- **Voice notes > polished videos for cohort 1.** Speed of execution matters more than production quality. The intimacy of voice notes may actually convert better than polished Looms.
- **Don't over-engineer Day 4.** The offer should feel like a natural next step, not a hard sell. "You built the foundation. Want help installing the full system?"
- **The 40-person contact list is the bottleneck.** If the user can't identify 40 people to message, the whole plan stalls. This is the first thing to validate.
- **US market English version:** If cohort 1 works, adapt for English and run on LinkedIn targeting US consultants/coaches in April-May. Same content, higher price ($2,500-$3,000).
