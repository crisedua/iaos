### Alex Hormozi — Offer Design & Acquisition

#### The Value Equation
**Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)**

To increase value: amplify the dream outcome, raise confidence it will work, shorten the time to results, and eliminate every ounce of friction and effort the client has to exert.

**Applied to SolIA:**
- Dream outcome: never miss a lead or appointment again — even at 2am on a Sunday
- Likelihood: live demo of a real voice agent on a real call (not a concept, a proof)
- Time delay: v1 live in days, not months
- Effort: zero — we handle everything (script, setup, testing, go-live)

**Applied to IA OS Director:**
- Dream outcome: work only on the things that only you can do — everything else runs on systems
- Likelihood: show the Context OS in action, show how a session replaces 3 hours of planning
- Time delay: first session produces a working system — same week
- Effort: you direct, Claude executes — no technical skill required

---

#### Grand Slam Offer
Don't sell a feature. Solve every problem standing between the client and their dream outcome. Stack the value until saying no feels irrational.

**How to build one:**
1. Name the dream outcome clearly ("never miss a lead again")
2. List every obstacle between them and that outcome
3. Package a solution to each obstacle into the offer
4. Price against the value delivered, not the cost to produce

**SolIA Grand Slam stack example:**
- Problem: misses calls after hours → Voice agent answers 24/7
- Problem: agent gives wrong info → Knowledge base built from their actual FAQ/website
- Problem: leads captured nowhere → Auto-log to Google Sheet or CRM
- Problem: hot lead cools off → Instant WhatsApp follow-up triggered on call end
- Problem: owner can't trust it → 1-week test period with real scenarios before go-live
- Problem: what if something breaks → Monthly support plan included for 60 days

All of that is one offer. Not "an AI agent." Never compete on features. Compete on certainty of outcome.

---

#### Pricing Philosophy
- Charge more, deliver more — premium price signals premium outcome
- Raising prices filters for serious buyers and reduces churn
- Price anchoring: show the cost of the problem (missed leads × avg deal value × months) before stating price
- Don't discount — add value instead (extend support, add a second workflow, include a training session)

---

#### Niche to Scale
Pick one industry first. Build one perfect Knowledge Base template, one perfect script, one perfect onboarding checklist for that niche. Get 2–3 case studies. Then expand.

**Current recommendation:** dental clinics or real estate — high inbound call volume, clear missed-call pain, appointment-based = measurable ROI.

#### Lead Magnets & Acquisition ($100M Leads)
Give away so much value that people want to hire you. The "Mini Diagnóstico IA" (already in `reference/mini-diagnostico-ia-os.md`) is a Hormozi-style lead magnet — a free 20-min session that uncovers pain and positions the paid offer naturally.

**Lead magnet principle:** The lead magnet should be the first step of the paid engagement, not a separate thing. If they complete it, they've already started — closing is just continuing.

### Dan Martell — Time, Delegation & the Operator Trap

#### The Operator Trap
Most business owners are trapped in execution — answering messages, doing admin, putting out fires. They're operators, not directors. The result: revenue is capped by their personal bandwidth. Nothing scales.

**This is the core pain IA OS Director solves.** When someone says "no tengo tiempo" or "sé lo que tengo que hacer pero no llego" — they are describing the operator trap.

The solution is not working harder. It's building systems (Context OS + AI + automations) that handle execution so the owner can direct.

---

#### Buyback Rate
Calculate your target hourly rate: (monthly revenue target) ÷ (hours/month available for work).

Any task that could be done by someone (or something) at a lower rate than your buyback rate should be offloaded. For clients: the AI agent, automation, or VA handles the below-rate work. The owner handles only what requires them.

**Applied to our own operations:**
- Cold call research on Google Maps → automate with `/extract-leads-gmap`
- Outreach scheduling and follow-up → `scripts/prepare-outreach.js`
- Lead logging and tracking → auto-append to `outreach-tracker.json`
- Anything not requiring our judgment → delegate or script it

---

#### DRIP Framework — What to Automate First
When doing an IA OS Director intake or SolIA discovery, identify tasks that are:
- **D**one before (repeated regularly)
- **R**epetitive (same process every time)
- **I**nefficient (takes longer than it should, prone to error)
- **P**ainful (dreaded, avoided, creates stress)

These are the highest-ROI automation targets. Use DRIP as a discovery tool in diagnostic calls — it surfaces pain and builds the roadmap simultaneously.

---

#### The Buyback Loop (for IA OS Director delivery)
1. **Audit** — map where the client's time actually goes (use `reference/ia-os-intake.md` Section 3)
2. **Transfer** — identify which tasks can be given to AI/automation/VA immediately
3. **Fill** — replace that time with high-leverage work only they can do (strategy, relationships, creative)

Repeat the loop every 90 days. Each cycle frees more time and creates more capacity for growth.

---

#### Camels Not Unicorns
Build a sustainable, profitable business — not a growth-at-all-costs machine. Focus on cash flow, margins, and repeatable revenue before scale.

**Applied to AI OS for Business:**
- Setup fees fund operations
- MRR (monthly support) is the camel's hump — the reserve that keeps you alive between deals
- Don't grow headcount or complexity until MRR covers base costs
- Prioritize retention over acquisition once pipeline is stable
